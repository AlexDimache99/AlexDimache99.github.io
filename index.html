Project 1 (Sea Life)

Here's the process I followed to design it:

1. Conceptualization: I began by thinking about the elements commonly associated with sea life, such as marine animals, waves, and aquatic plants. The goal was to create a design that captures the essence of the ocean.

2. Sketching: I drafted several rough sketches to explore different layouts. This stage was all about experimenting with various ideas and combinations of sea life elements.

3. Choosing a Design Style: I opted for a clean and modern style that incorporates fluid lines and shapes to represent the dynamic nature of the sea. A balanced composition ensures that the logo is visually appealing and professional.

4. Color Palette: I chose a color palette inspired by the ocean, using shades of blue and green to evoke a sense of tranquility and depth.

5. Digital Design: Using digital design tools, I translated the chosen sketch into a digital format. This involved refining the shapes, adjusting proportions, and ensuring the logo is scalable for various uses.

6. Feedback and Refinement: I reviewed the digital design, making necessary adjustments to improve the overall look and feel. This step ensured that the logo was clear, cohesive, and aesthetically pleasing.

7. Finalization: Once satisfied with the design, I finalized the logo, making sure it works well in different sizes and on various backgrounds.

Project 2 (Library)

Here's the process I followed to design it:

1. Conceptualization: The journey began with brainstorming ideas and elements that are commonly associated with libraries, such as books, shelves, and open pages. The goal was to create a logo that represents knowledge, learning, and community.

2. Sketching: I created several rough sketches to explore different layouts and compositions. This stage was all about experimenting with various ideas and visual elements to see what works best.

3. Choosing a Design Style: I opted for a clean and modern style, incorporating elegant lines and shapes to reflect the professionalism and welcoming nature of a library. A balanced composition ensures that the logo is visually appealing and easy to recognize.

4. Color Palette: Inspired by the serene and intellectual atmosphere of libraries, I chose a color palette featuring neutral and calming tones, such as shades of blue and grey, which evoke a sense of tranquility and focus.

5. Digital Design: Using design software, I translated the chosen sketch into a digital format. This involved refining the shapes, adjusting proportions, and ensuring the logo is scalable for various uses, from library cards to large banners.

6. Feedback and Refinement: I reviewed the digital design to make necessary adjustments and enhance its overall appearance. This step ensured that the logo was clear, cohesive, and aesthetically pleasing.

7. Finalization: Once satisfied with the design, I finalized the logo, making sure it works well in different sizes and on various backgrounds, ensuring versatility and usability.
